Who We Are

A Legal Think Tank
for the Digital Era

Digital Law and Policy Centre Pvt. Ltd. is a Kathmandu-based legal services and research organisation incorporated under the laws of Nepal. We sit at the intersection of traditional legal practice and emerging digital governance — bringing rigour to questions of law, rights, and public policy.

We work with individuals, civil society organisations, government bodies, and international institutions to advance legal literacy, provide expert counsel, and conduct research that shapes policy across Nepal and the broader South Asian region.

Our work spans court representation, legal documentation, investor facilitation, public interest advocacy, and the growing field of digital rights — grounded in Nepal's domestic legal framework and international human rights standards.

Nepal Digital Law Monitor

A living tracker of Nepal's digital governance legislation — existing laws, pending bills, and identified policy gaps across platform regulation, privacy, cybersecurity, and data protection.

Sources: MoCIT · Digital Rights Nepal 2024 Report · Pioneer Law Associates · Nepal Economic Forum

⚠
September 2025 — Nepal blocks 26 social media platforms including Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube, X, and LinkedIn for failure to register under the Social Media Directive 2023. TikTok, Viber, and several domestic platforms remain operational. The Social Media Act (Bill) 2081 is pending parliamentary approval and has drawn criticism from UNESCO, IFJ, and civil society groups.
In Force · Foundational
● In Force
Electronic Transactions Act, 2063 (2008)
विद्युतीय (इलेक्ट्रोनिक) कारोबार सम्बन्धी ऐन, २०६३
Nepal's primary digital law. Governs electronic records, digital signatures, and cybercrime. Originally for early internet transactions — now stretched to cover social media regulation through Section 79 (basis for the Social Media Directive). Widely regarded as outdated and in need of replacement.
Gap
Does not cover data protection, AI governance, platform liability, or algorithmic transparency. Requires complete replacement.
In Force · Privacy
● In Force
Individual Privacy Act, 2075 (2018)
वैयक्तिक गोपनियता सम्बन्धी ऐन, २०७५
Nepal's primary privacy statute. Protects personal data including electronic data, requires consent for collection and use of personal information. Provides the right to be forgotten in digital contexts. Contains no dedicated Data Protection Authority, no breach notification requirement, and no cross-border transfer rules.
Gap
No DPA. No provisions on cookies or profiling. Does not meet GDPR adequacy standards — critical given India's DPDP Act's cross-border scope affecting Nepali data processors.
In Force · Platform Regulation
● In Force
Social Media Directive (DMSM, 2080) 2023
सामाजिक सञ्जाल सञ्चालन, प्रयोग तथा नियमन निर्देशिका, २०८०
Cabinet-enacted November 2023 under ETA Section 79. Requires all social media platforms to register with MoCIT, appoint local grievance officers, and moderate content in Nepali. The September 2025 blocking of 26 platforms including Facebook, WhatsApp, and YouTube was enforced under this directive following Supreme Court direction (Case 080-CF-0012).
Concern
No judicial oversight for content removal. Vague prohibited content categories risk suppressing legitimate speech. No safe harbour provision for platforms.
Pending Parliament · 2025
◐ Pending Parliament
Social Media Act (Bill), 2081
सामाजिक सञ्जाल सञ्चालन, प्रयोग तथा नियमन सम्बन्धी विधेयक, २०८१
Registered at Nepal's National Assembly 28 January 2025. Proposes mandatory licensing for all platforms, government authority to revoke licences on national security grounds, penalties up to NPR 3 lakh or 2 years imprisonment for hate speech and impersonation, prohibition on anonymous accounts. UNESCO analysis (17 March 2025) flagged incompatibility with international freedom of expression standards.
Key Concern
No safe harbour. Criminalises anonymous speech. Overbroad content categories. Overlaps with IT & Cybersecurity Bill 2024. Criticised by UNESCO, IFJ, RSP, and major civil society groups.
Draft · Under Review · 2024
◐ Under Review
IT and Cybersecurity Bill, 2080 (2024)
सूचना प्रविधि तथा साइबर सुरक्षा विधेयक, २०८०
Introduced to replace the Electronic Transactions Act. Aims to enhance cybersecurity, regulate critical infrastructure, require annual security audits, and govern electronic public services. Contains progressive provisions but also raises concerns about overbroad restrictions on online expression and arbitrary surveillance clauses. Duplicates social media licensing provisions in the Social Media Bill.
Gap
Lacks cross-border cooperation mechanisms equivalent to Budapest Convention. Must resolve duplication with Social Media Act before enactment.
In Force · Taxation
● In Force
Digital Service Tax & VAT on Digital Services
Finance Act 2079 (2022) · VAT Act Amendment
2% Digital Service Tax on non-resident digital service providers (Finance Act 2079, Section 20). Amendments to VAT Act require global platforms exceeding annual transaction threshold to register with IRD and collect 13% VAT. In FY 2023/24, 18 registered firms paid NPR 416.71 million in combined digital taxes on annual turnover of NPR 3 billion.
Status
Operationally active. Meta, Google, and X compliance status under scrutiny following the September 2025 platform ban. Tax revenue collection impacted by unregistered status of major platforms.
Identified Gap
✗ No Law Exists
Comprehensive Data Protection Law
No standalone legislation · 2025
Nepal has no dedicated Data Protection Authority or comprehensive data protection statute equivalent to GDPR or India's DPDP Act 2023. The Privacy Act 2018 provides partial coverage but lacks: dedicated DPA, data breach notification, data subject rights (portability, erasure), cross-border transfer rules, and sector-specific obligations. India's DPDP Act creates cross-border implications for Nepali data processors handling Indian citizen data.
Recommended Action
Amend Privacy Act 2018 with GDPR-aligned provisions, or enact standalone data protection legislation with an independent supervisory authority.
Identified Gap
✗ Not Acceded
Budapest Convention on Cybercrime
Council of Europe · 2001 · Nepal not a party
The Budapest Convention is the only binding international treaty on cybercrime, facilitating cross-border cooperation in evidence gathering and prosecution. Nepal has not acceded. As cybercrime increasingly crosses borders — ransomware, online fraud, sextortion affecting Nepali victims — the absence of a formal framework creates serious enforcement gaps.
Recommended Action
Nepal should formally assess accession to the Budapest Convention to enable mutual legal assistance for cross-border cybercrime cases. Critical given growing digital crime reported in Nepali communities abroad.
